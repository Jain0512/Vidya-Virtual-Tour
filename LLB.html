LLB


The LLB (Bachelor of Laws) is a professional undergraduate law degree that provides students with a comprehensive understanding of legal principles, frameworks, and practices. The program typically spans three years for those who have completed a bachelor's degree. It covers a broad range of subjects including constitutional law, criminal law, contract law, civil procedure, and legal ethics. The course emphasizes critical thinking, legal writing, and practical skills such as advocacy and research. Students also gain exposure to real-world legal issues through internships, moot courts, and legal aid activities. An LLB degree prepares graduates for diverse career paths in legal practice, corporate law, government, and beyond.

Career Opportunities


The LLB degree opens a wide range of career paths, from traditional legal practice to roles in the corporate, government, academic, and non-profit sectors.
